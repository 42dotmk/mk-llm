The Elder Scrolls series is a popular and long-running series of action role-playing open-world video games developed by Bethesda Game Studios and published by Bethesda Softworks. The games take place on the fictional continent of Tamriel, in which the Elder Scrolls universe is set. Tamriel contains nine provinces, each with its own unique history, landscape, cultures, and politics. Throughout the series, different games are primarily set within a single province or explore various provinces.

Below is a list of the major games in the series and the primary provinces they are set in:

1. The Elder Scrolls: Arena (1994) - Arena takes place across the entire continent of Tamriel, making it a unique entry in the series, as players can visit all the regions in the game. The main story revolves around the efforts to free Emperor Uriel Septim VII from imprisonment in Oblivion and restore him to the Imperial throne.

2. The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall (1996) - Daggerfall is set in the provinces of High Rock and Hammerfell, which are located in the northwest of Tamriel. Primarily focusing on the regions of Iliac Bay and the eponymous city of Daggerfall, the story centers on the player investigating the supernatural events and political intrigue involving various factions vying for power.

3. The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind (2002) - Morrowind takes place on the island of Vvardenfell, in the province of Morrowind, which is located in the northeast of Tamriel. The main quest revolves around the protagonist's role as the Nerevarine, a reincarnation of the ancient hero Indoril Nerevar, and the impending doom of the island due to the rise of Dagoth Ur, an exiled god.

4. The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion (2006) - Oblivion is set in the province of Cyrodiil, the heartland of Tamriel and home of the Imperial City, which is the capital of the entire continent. The main storyline follows the player's hero after the assassination of Emperor Uriel Septim VII and stopping the invasion of demonic creatures from the realm of Oblivion, led by the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon.

5. The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim (2011) - Skyrim takes place in the northern province of Skyrim, home to the Nords. The game's main plot revolves around the return of the dragons, led by the ancient dragon Alduin, who intends to bring about the apocalypse. As the Dragonborn, the player character is destined to fight and eliminate this threat.

6. The Elder Scrolls Online (2014) - The Elder Scrolls Online (ESO) is a massively multiplayer online game that covers regions across the entire continent of Tamriel, including provinces from the previous games like High Rock, Morrowind, Skyrim, and Cyrodiil. The game's main storyline revolves around defeating the Daedric Prince Molag Bal, who seeks to merge Tamriel with his plane of Oblivion.

In summary, the primary settings for the Elder Scrolls games are different provinces within the continent of Tamriel, exploring different regions, cultures, and histories in each edition. The series allows players to immerse themselves in the rich lore and environments crafted by Bethesda Game Studios, making Tamriel one of the most beloved game worlds in the history of role-playing games.
