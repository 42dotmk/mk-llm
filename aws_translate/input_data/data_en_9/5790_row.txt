The Lufthansa Group Airlines is one of the leading aviation groups in the world, and comprises several major airlines from different European countries. The group is known for its extensive route network, high-quality services, and reliable operations. The following are the five main carriers that are part of the Lufthansa Group Airlines:

1. Lufthansa: Lufthansa is the largest carrier within the Lufthansa Group Airlines and is also the flagship entity of the group. This German airline was founded in 1953 and is based in Cologne, Germany. Lufthansa operates a vast global network, serving numerous destinations across Europe, Africa, Americas, Asia, and the Middle East. Their fleet comprises a mix of Airbus and Boeing aircraft, and they are especially known for their extensive operations with the Airbus A380 and Boeing 747. As one of the founding members of the Star Alliance, Lufthansa is committed to offering seamless connectivity and a world of travel benefits to their passengers.

2. SWISS: Swiss International Air Lines, commonly referred to as SWISS, is the national airline of Switzerland and is another key member of the Lufthansa Group Airlines. Headquartered in Basel, Switzerland, SWISS was founded in 2002 as the successor to the now-defunct Swissair. This carrier primarily serves Europe, North and South America, Africa, and Asia with a fleet that includes Airbus and Bombardier aircraft. SWISS is known for its emphasis on quality, reliability, and outstanding service. It is also a Star Alliance member airline.

3. Austrian Airlines: Austrian Airlines is the flag carrier airline of Austria and is based in Vienna. Established in 1957, it became a part of the Lufthansa Group Airlines in 2009. Austrian Airlines operates a comprehensive network of routes, covering various destinations in Europe, the Middle East, Asia, Africa, and North America. Their fleet consists of both Airbus and Boeing aircraft, as well as Bombardier Q Series for regional flights. Like the other members of the group, Austrian Airlines is also a member of the Star Alliance, further expanding its connectivity and services.

4. Brussels Airlines: Brussels Airlines is the largest airline of Belgium and operates from its hub in Brussels Airport. Established in 2006 as a merger of SN Brussels Airlines and Virgin Express, it joined the Lufthansa Group Airlines in 2016. Brussels Airlines primarily serves destinations across Europe, Africa, and North America. Their fleet comprises different types of aircraft, including Airbus, Bombardier, and the Sukhoi Superjet. As a part of their commitment to customer service, Brussels Airlines is also a member of the Star Alliance.

5. Eurowings: Eurowings is a low-cost airline and a crucial subsidiary of the Lufthansa Group Airlines. Established in 1993, it began operations as a regional carrier before evolving into a low-cost airline in 2014.
