1. HP OfficeJet 4620 AirPrint vs HP Color LaserJet Enterprise M750n:

Printing Technology:
The HP OfficeJet 4620 Airprint is an inkjet printer providing print resolution of up to 4800 x 1200 dpi for color and 1200 x 600 dpi for black. It employs Thermal Inkjet technology for its prints.
On the other hand, the HP Color LaserJet Enterprise M750n is a laser printer that offers up to 600 x 600 dpi for both color and black printing, using a laser printing mechanism, offering better text printing quality.

Functionality:
While the OfficeJet 4620 is an all-in-one printer providing print, scan, copy, and faxing capabilities, the LaserJet M750n is solely focused on printing.

Print Speed:
The HP OfficeJet 4620 has a print speed of 8 pages per minute (ppm) for black and 7.5 ppm for color prints. The HP Color LaserJet Enterprise M750n boasts much faster print speeds of 30 ppm for black and color prints.

Paper Handling:
The HP OfficeJet 4620 supports standard input capacity of 80 sheets, while the LaserJet Enterprise M750n has a much higher input capacity of 850 sheets, expandable up to 2350 sheets with optional trays, making it better suited for enterprise scenarios requiring high volume printing.

Wireless capabilities:
The HP OfficeJet 4620 supports AirPrint technology and mobile printing, whereas the HP Color LaserJet Enterprise M750n doesn't support wireless printing without an additional accessory.

Price:
The HP OfficeJet 4620 is much more affordable compared to the HP Color LaserJet M750n. The latter being a printer targeted more towards enterprise and high-volume printing needs.

The OfficeJet 4620 offers a print resolution of up to 4800 x 1200 dpi for color and 1200 x 600 dpi for black, while the DeskJet 1112 offers up to 4800 x 1200 dpi for color and 1200 x 1200 dpi for black.

Paper Handling:

Wireless capabilities:

Price:
The HP DeskJet 1112 is a much more affordable option as compared to the HP OfficeJet 4620 but compromises on functionality and features for the lower cost.
