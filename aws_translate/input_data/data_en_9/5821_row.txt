B. Yes, both questions 1 and 2 are asking the same thing, which is how to write a letter excusing your child's absence from school. In order to address this topic, I will provide a detailed guideline on how to write the absence letter.

1. Heading: Start with your name, contact details (address, phone number, and email address), and the date at the top left corner of the letter.

2. Addressee: Write the name of the school principal, the school's name, and its address. If you know the specific person to whom you are addressing the letter, include their name and designation.

3. Subject Line: Write a brief and clear subject line that mentions the main purpose of the letter, such as "Excused Absence for [Child's Name]" or "Request to Excuse Absence for [Child's Name]."

4. Salutation: Greet the recipient with a formal salutation, such as "Dear Principal [Last Name]" or "Dear [Designation/Title]."

5. Introduction: With a clear and concise tone, clarify who you are and your main reason for writing the letter. Mention your child's name, grade, and class (if applicable). Briefly state the reason for the absence and the dates your child was/will be absent from school.

6. Reason for Absence: Explain the reason for your child's absence in more detail. If the absence is due to illness, mention the doctor's visit or recommendations, without going into too many specific medical details. If the absence is due to a family event or travel plans, explain the importance of the event or trip. Always maintain a respectful tone.

7. Offer Support: Offer assistance to the school by ensuring your child will complete any missed assignments, homework, or exams. Request information on any additional work or materials your child needs to catch up on.

8. Express Gratitude: Thank the school staff and teachers for their cooperation and understanding. Also, request that they notify you if there are any specific actions required for your child's absence to be officially excused.

9. Closing: End the letter with a formal closing, such as "Sincerely," "Kind Regards," or "Yours Faithfully," followed by your full name and signature.

10. Proofread: Double-check the letter for grammar, punctuation, and spelling mistakes. Make sure the information is accurate and clear.
