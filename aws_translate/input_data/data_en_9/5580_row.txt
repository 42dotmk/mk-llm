David Akers: The Journey from Rookie to Pro-Bowler

Born on December 9, 1974, in Lexington, Kentucky, David Roy Akers would grow up to become one of the most accurate and consistent placekickers in NFL history. Although his initial entry to the NFL was unassuming - as an undrafted free agent in 1997 - Akers' perseverance and unwavering dedication to his craft would soon set him on a path to greatness.

He began his career with the Atlanta Falcons before joining the Washington Redskins for a season. However, it was with the Philadelphia Eagles in 1999 when things began to look up for Akers. From 1999 to 2010, Akers was a staple on the Eagles' roster, participating in five Pro Bowls and achieving two All-Pro honors. After a successful run in Philadelphia, he played for the San Francisco 49ers for two seasons, and he concluded his career with the Detroit Lions in 2013.

A Field Goal Tally to Remember

Throughout Akers' illustrious 16-year NFL career, he was no stranger to making high-pressure kicks with formidable accuracy. So, how many field goals did he make in his tenure?

Examining the available statistics on David Akers' professional career, we find that he accumulated a total of 386 successful field goals, out of 477 attempted kicks. This impressive track record places Akers 12th on the all-time list of successful field goals made in NFL history. His field goal percentage of 80.9% demonstrates his remarkable consistency and reliability throughout his career.

Akers' regular-season field goals were not the only highlight of his career. David also had a penchant for shining bright in playoff matches. During his postseason appearances, Akers successfully completed 39 field goals out of 45 attempts. This record made him the all-time leader for postseason field goals before he was surpassed by Adam Vinatieri and Stephen Gostkowski.
