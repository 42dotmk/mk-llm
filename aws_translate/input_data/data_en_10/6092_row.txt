Arrabbiata Sauce:
Arrabbiata sauce, originating in Italy, is a popular spicy sauce used in Italian cuisine, mainly served with pasta. The word "Arrabbiata" means "angry" in Italian, which refers to the spicy nature of the sauce. The primary ingredients include tomatoes, garlic, red chili peppers, and olive oil. This sauce is typical of the Lazio region, around Rome, but is popular and enjoyed throughout Italy and the world.
