1. What do Xs mean on a datasheet truth table?

In a truth table, commonly used in digital electronics and computer science, 'X' is used to denote a "don't care" condition. This means that the specific value (either 0 or 1) at that position in the truth table does not affect the outcome of the output function. In other words, it doesn't matter whether the value is high or low, true or false, 0 or 1; the outcome remains the same.

Truth tables are essential tools for describing and representing the inputs and outputs of digital circuits and logic functions, such as AND, OR, and NOT gates. In some cases, certain input combinations may not have any functional consequence, or designers may choose to simplify circuits by ignoring some input combinations. In these instances, 'X' is used to symbolize the "don't care" input condition, which greatly simplifies the design, testing, or verification process of these circuits.
