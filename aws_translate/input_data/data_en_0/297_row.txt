Getting mud off your shoes may not be the most exciting task, but it's certainly necessary to maintain cleanliness in your home and the longevity of your footwear. Here are detailed steps on two methods you can use:

Method 1: Dry Cleaning

1. Allow the Mud to Dry: It might sound counter-intuitive, but allowing the mud to fully dry can make it easier to remove. Attempting to clean away wet mud may only serve to spread it and deepen the stain. In order to speed up the drying process, place your shoes in a warm, dry spot. However, avoid placing them on a heater or in strong sunlight, as these can damage the material of your shoes.

2. Scrape off the Mud: Once the mud is dry, you can begin the process of removing it. Using an old toothbrush, a nail file or a specialised shoe brush, gently scrape off the mud. Be careful to avoid damaging the shoe material. Always brush in the same direction to prevent unnecessary wear.

3. Clean the Outsoles: Pay extra attention to the outsoles, or the bottom part of your shoes. This area typically gathers the most dirt and debris. Use your tool to scrape the mud from grooves or patterns in the shoes.

4. Wipe with a Dry Cloth: After you've scraped off as much mud as possible, wipe over your shoes with a clean, dry cloth to get rid of any lingering dust.

Method 2: Wet Cleaning

1. Preparing the Shoes: If the mud is too wet or if the dry cleaning method is not enough, you may need to follow a wet cleaning method. Before you start this, make sure to remove the laces and insoles if possible to prevent them from getting wet and to clean more thoroughly.

2. Rinse Your Shoes: Using warm water, thoroughly rinse the mud off your shoes. Avoid using high pressure streams as it might damage the material of your shoes.

3. Create a Cleaning Solution: In a bowl, mix warm water with a few drops of mild detergent or dish soap.

4. Clean your Shoes: Dip a soft brush or an old toothbrush into the cleaning solution and start scrubbing the mud spots gently. Ensure not to forget the sides and tops of your shoes. 

5. Rinse and Dry: Rinse your shoes again with warm water to remove soap suds and residual mud. Pat them dry using a towel to remove as much moisture as possible, then leave them to air dry in a warm spot avoiding direct sunlight.

6. Conditioning: After thorough drying, apply some conditioner if the shoes material permits, to restore its natural shine and prevent cracking.

Remember, if your shoes are made of a special material like suede or leather, they may need a specific shoe cleaning product instead of mild detergent, and more care will be needed when scrubbing.

Both methods can effectively remove mud from your shoes. However, consider the type of shoes, the materials made from, and your comfort level with the cleaning process when deciding which method to use. In some cases, scraping off dried mud might be sufficient and convenient. For harder stains or more durable shoes, a wet clean might be more effective.
