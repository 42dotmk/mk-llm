Geographically, Romania is the 12th largest country in Europe, covering an area of 238,391 square kilometers. It is bordered by five countries, namely Bulgaria, Serbia, Hungary, Ukraine, and Moldova. The Carpathian Mountains run through the center of the country, which is surrounded by the fertile plains of the Danube River and the Black Sea coast. Romania is home to various natural wonders, including dense forests, glacier lakes, and the Danube Delta - the second largest and best-preserved delta in Europe.

The population of Romania is estimated to be around 19.3 million people as of 2021. The majority of the population is ethnic Romanians (around 89%), while the largest minority group is the Hungarians, followed by the Romani people. The official language is Romanian, which is a Romance language closely related to Italian, French, and Spanish. The predominant religion in Romania is Eastern Orthodox Christianity.

Romania's culture carries a rich history of influences from its neighboring regions and beyond, including the ancient Dacians, Romans, Slavs, Ottomans, and the Austro-Hungarian Empire. Traditional Romanian arts, music, dance, and cuisine all bear traces of these influences, making it a unique cultural destination. The country's folklore is rich in mythical creatures, tales, and superstitions, which continue to hold a strong presence in present-day Romanian culture. Notable historical landmarks in Romania include the Bran Castle, also known as "Dracula's Castle," and the Painted Monasteries of Bucovina, both of which draw tourists from around the world.

Romania's modern history is marked by a relatively recent transition from a communist regime to a democratic government following the fall of Nicolae Ceausescu in 1989 and its accession to the European Union in 2007. The political system is a semi-presidential parliamentary republic, with a president, prime minister, and a bicameral Parliament composed of the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies. Romania is also a member of international organizations such as NATO, the United Nations, and the World Trade Organization.

The economy of Romania has experienced significant growth and development since the fall of communism and continues to evolve as an upper-middle-income country. Romania has a mixed economy, with services, industry, and agriculture contributing a large portion to its GDP. Romania is an important agricultural producer in Europe, with major crops such as wheat, corn, and sunflower seeds. The country is also rich in natural resources, including oil, natural gas, coal, and gold. Additionally, the technology and IT sectors have experienced rapid growth in recent years, with Romanian entrepreneurs and startups gaining international recognition.
