The United States is a vast and diverse country with numerous large cities spread across its territory. The "top 10 biggest cities" could be interpreted in two ways: by population or by physical size (land area). Below, I provide both lists for your information.

Top 10 Biggest U.S. Cities by Population (Source: U.S. Census Bureau, 2020):

1. New York City, New York - With a population of approximately 8,804,190, New York City is the most populous city in the United States. It is a global center of finance, media, fashion, and entertainment and is made up of five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island.

2. Los Angeles, California - With a population of about 3,898,747, Los Angeles is the second-most populous city in the U.S. It is known for its entertainment industry, particularly Hollywood, as well as its sunny climate and expansive beaches.

3. Chicago, Illinois - With a population of around 2,697,000, Chicago is the third-largest city in the country. Nicknamed the "Windy City," Chicago is a major hub for industry, transportation, and culture.

4. Houston, Texas - With a population of approximately 2,316,000, Houston is the fourth-most populous city in the U.S. It is known for its role in the energy industry and as home to NASA's Johnson Space Center.

5. Phoenix, Arizona - With a population of around 1,708,000, Phoenix ranks as the fifth-largest city in the United States. It is known for its desert climate, golf courses, and outdoor recreational opportunities.

6. Philadelphia, Pennsylvania - With a population of approximately 1,579,000, Philadelphia is the sixth-most populous city in the country. It is one of the oldest cities in the U.S. and played a key role in American history.

7. San Antonio, Texas - With a population of around 1,567,000, San Antonio is the seventh-largest city in the United States. The city is known for its rich history, including the historic sites of the Alamo and the San Antonio Missions.

8. San Diego, California - With a population of approximately 1,402,000, San Diego ranks as the eighth-largest city in the U.S. Known for its beautiful beaches, mild climate, and major tourist attractions like the San Diego Zoo and Balboa Park.

9. Dallas, Texas - With a population of around 1,343,000, Dallas is the ninth-largest city in the country. The city plays a significant role in the telecommunications and technology sectors and is home to numerous Fortune 500 companies.

10. San Jose, California - With a population of approximately 1,013,000, San Jose ranks as the tenth-largest city in the United States. It is located in the heart of Silicon Valley and is a major center for tech and innovation.
