Lance Bass is a multifaceted individual who has taken on various professional roles throughout his career. These roles include being a singer, actor, producer, writer, and entrepreneur. Here is an in-depth look at the different aspects of his professional life:

1. Music Career - Lance Bass first gained international fame as a member of the popular singing group NSYNC. Formed in 1995, NSYNC went on to become one of the best-selling boy bands in history and played a major role in shaping Lance's career. The group released several hit albums, like "No Strings Attached" and "Celebrity," and topped the charts with several single tracks. Although NSYNC went on an indefinite hiatus in 2002, Lance's association with the group solidified his place in the entertainment industry.

2. Acting - After the success of NSYNC, Lance Bass ventured into the world of acting. He has appeared in various film and television projects in both guest-starring and lead roles. Some of his film appearances include "On the Line" (2001), "Zoolander" (2001), "I Now Pronounce You Chuck & Larry" (2007), and "Kathy Griffin: My Life on the D-List" (2005). He has also lent his voice to characters in animated series like "Kim Possible" (2005) and "Rick & Steve: The Happiest Gay Couple in All the World" (2007).

3. Producing - Lance has shown a keen interest in production and has been associated with different television and film projects in various capacities. He founded his own production company called Lance Bass Productions, which produced documentaries like "Kidnapped for Christ" (2014) and "The Boy Band Con: The Lou Pearlman Story" (2019). He has also served as an executive producer for various TV shows, including "Finding Prince Charming" (2016-2017).

4. Writing - Lance is also a published author, with his autobiography "Out of Sync" released in 2007. The memoir covers his life, focusing particularly on his time in NSYNC, personal struggles, and coming out as gay.

5. Entrepreneurship - In addition to his entertainment ventures, Lance has been involved in several business opportunities. He has invested in various companies in different industries, including technology, food, fashion, and entertainment. He is a co-owner of the West Hollywood restaurant, Rocco's WeHo, which opened in 2019.

6. Television Hosting - Lance has worked as a television host for numerous programs, including the SiriusXM radio show "Dirty Pop with Lance Bass" from 2013-2017. In 2017, he hosted the television series "90's House," alongside Christina Milian, on MTV.

7. Philanthropy and Activism - Lance is highly regarded for his philanthropic efforts and activism. He has worked with various charitable organizations, such as The Trevor Project, which focuses on suicide prevention among LGBTQ+ youth, and the Environmental Media Association, which promotes environmental sustainability through entertainment. Additionally, Lance has been a strong advocate for LGBTQ+ rights and equality.

In summary, Lance Bass's multifaceted career spans music, acting, production, writing, entrepreneurship, television hosting, and philanthropy. His versatile talent, hard work, and dedication to various causes have made him an influential figure in the entertainment industry and beyond.
